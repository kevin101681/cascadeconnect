Your Role as Homeowner

We're partners in maintaining your home. Here's what you're responsible for:

  • Routine Maintenance: Changing furnace filters, cleaning gutters, caulking, etc.
  • Preventing Damage: Shutting off water during leaks, clearing snow from drains
  • Providing Access: Being present for inspections and repairs (adult required)
  • Timely Reporting: Submitting claims before warranty expires
Important: Maintenance vs. Warranty

Warranty covers defects in materials or workmanship. It does NOT cover:

  • Damage from neglected maintenance (dirty filters causing furnace failure)
  • Normal wear and tear (carpet matting in high-traffic areas)
  • Homeowner-caused damage (cracked tile from dropped object)
  • Natural settling (minor hairline drywall cracks)

When in doubt, submit it anyway — we'll help you determine coverage!

EMERGENCY: No Heat

Use this checklist when outdoor temperature is below 50°F and you have no heat.

Complete ALL Steps Before Calling

STEP 1: Check Your Thermostat

  • Set to "HEAT" mode (not "COOL" or "OFF")
  • Temperature set at least 2° ABOVE current room temperature
  • Replace batteries if display is dim (most use 2 AA batteries)

STEP 2: Check Furnace Power

  • Find furnace (basement, utility room, or closet)
  • Light switch on side of furnace is ON (may be upside down)
  • Door panel is fully closed and clicked into place (furnace won't run if loose)

STEP 3: Check Electrical Panel

  • Find breaker labeled "FURNACE" or "HVAC"
  • If tripped, flip to OFF first, then back to ON

STEP 4: Check Furnace Filter

  • Open furnace door, remove air filter
  • Hold it up to light — can you see through it?
  • If NO = Replace immediately (change every 3 months)

STEP 5: Check Airflow

  • All room vents are OPEN (not blocked by furniture)
  • Cold air return vents are UNBLOCKED

STEP 6: For Gas Furnaces Only

  • Gas valve at meter is OPEN (handle parallel to pipe)
  • Gas valve at furnace is OPEN (usually red, handle parallel)
STILL NO HEAT? CALL NOW

Cascade Builder Services: 888.429.5468 (24/7)
Have ready: Your address, outdoor temperature, which steps you completed

EMERGENCY: Plumbing Leaks

MAJOR LEAK (Water Everywhere)

DO THIS IMMEDIATELY:

STEP 1: SHUT OFF WATER

  • Option A: Main shut-off inside home (turn clockwise)
  • Option B: Water meter at street (need "curb key" tool)

STEP 2: PROTECT BELONGINGS

  • Move furniture/electronics away from water
  • Use towels/buckets to contain spread

STEP 3: CALL FOR HELP

  • Under 30 days in home? Call CBS: 888.429.5468
  • Over 30 days? Call plumber + homeowner's insurance

MINOR LEAK (One Fixture)

STEP 1: Stop Water Flow

  • Find shut-off valve under sink/toilet
  • Turn clockwise until water stops

STEP 2: Check Simple Causes

  • Missing/cracked caulking around tub/shower?
  • Shower door left open during use?
  • Loose connection under sink?

STEP 3: Dry & Report

  • Wipe up all standing water
  • Use other facilities in your home
  • Submit warranty claim during business hours (NOT an emergency)

Homeowner's Glossary

We've translated construction jargon into plain English. Bookmark this page!

Asphalt Shingles
Common roofing material with protective granules that look like sand
Ball Valve
A shut-off valve with a handle you turn 90 degrees (usually for gas or water)
Caulking
Flexible sealing compound used to fill gaps and prevent water entry
Circuit Breaker
Safety switch in your electrical panel that shuts off power if overloaded
Control Joint
Planned crack line in concrete that controls where natural cracking occurs
Curb Key
Special wrench used to turn off water at the street meter (available at hardware stores)
GFCI Outlet
Safety outlet with "test" and "reset" buttons (required in bathrooms/kitchens) that shuts off power if water is detected
Hose Bib
Outdoor water faucet where you attach your garden hose
P-Trap
U-shaped pipe under sinks that holds water to block sewer gases from entering home
Sump Pump
Pump in basement that removes excess groundwater to prevent flooding
Swale
Shallow drainage ditch designed to direct rainwater away from your home
